What the Collation Revealed

Finding One: The Corruption of GM 1906

The short version: The 1906 print edition is not Mah Laqa Bai's text. It is an editor's reconstruction.

Chronological proof: SJ 1818 was commissioned by Mah Laqa herself and completed six years before her death. It is fully Dakhni. If she had revised toward northern Urdu, that revision would appear here. It does not.

Shared scribal error: In Ghazal 69.3, SJ and GM share the corrupted reading مرا (merā) for مزا (mazā), while BL preserves the correct reading. This provides the Lachmannian proof that GM is a direct derivative of SJ.

Statistical proof: From the couplet-level Levenshtein distance matrix built for this edition:

A poet revising her own work over nineteen years (1799–1818) changed a maximum of 8.2% of couplets substantially. An editor in 1906 changed 50.2% substantially to enforce early-twentieth-century linguistic purity.

Structural Evidence: The most radical proof is Ghazal SJ-43, a panegyric to Raja Rao Ranbha where his name serves as the implied radīf. The 1906 editor, mistaking the implied radīf for a metrical defect, completely overwrote the poem, erasing the historical patron entirely:

SJ 1818 (Authentic Text)

جوں عمر خضر کا ہو اجمال راؤ رنبھا
تیری گرہ ہو ایسی ہر سال راؤ رنبھا

GM 1906 (Corrupted Print)

ہو تری عمر خضر ماہ جمال
یوں ہی تیری گرہ ہو سال بہ سال

Finding Two: The Dakhni Question

For over a century, scholars assumed Mah Laqa Bai revised her own Dakhni poetry toward northern Urdu to please the Asaf Jahi court. The manuscript evidence restores Mah Laqa Bai to the position previously denied to her: a Dakhni poet who never abandoned her linguistic heritage. Previous scholars were not careless; they simply had the wrong text.

(یہ تمام محققین نے جو کچھ لکھا وہ ان کے سامنے موجود متن کی روشنی میں درست تھا۔ لیکن وہ متن ماہ لقا کا نہیں تھا۔)

Prosodic Normalization: The early Deccani tradition frequently compressed the pronoun کوئی (koī) into a single long syllable (فَعْ). Mah Laqa Bai used this archaic scansion in nine couplets. The 1906 editor, viewing this as a metrical defect, rewrote the syntax of all nine lines. The retention rate for this specific Deccani scansion in the print edition is exactly 0.0%.
