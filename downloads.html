The Editorial Methodology

1. Base Text Selection

The Salar Jung Museum manuscript dated **SJ 1818** serves as the definitive base text for this critical edition. Codicological analysis establishes it as the last surviving compilation prepared under the direct authorial supervision of Mah Laqa Bai Chanda before her passing, making it her permanent, intended historical monument. It provides the most mathematically stable, textually complete matrix at 125 ghazals, showing unbroken continuity with earlier witnesses spanning a period of nineteen years.

2. Emendation & Bracket Policy [...]

The textual reconstruction follows a conservative emendation strategy. The base text is altered exclusively in instances of clear mechanical disruption, scribal slips, or severe physical degradation. Where words or individual syllables are missing from the base manuscript due to paper damage or omissions, they are programmatically supplied from the alternative historical witnesses (such as the early recension branches **BL 1799** or **TO 1811**).

To preserve complete, unadulterated analytical integrity, all such editorially supplied interventions are explicitly contained within square brackets, for example: [لفظ].

Computational Instructions for Data Analysis:

The bracketed markers are carefully retained inside the raw dataset files to give computer scientists and computational philologists complete choice over their pipeline:

• To Analyze as Structural Diffs (Run As-Is): If you run text-comparison, distance, or phylogenetic algorithms on the raw matrix as-is, string-matching loops will naturally recognize the bracketed strings as distinct mutations (i.e., treating [لفظ] as not equal to لفظ). This treats editorial additions as explicit character deviations.

• To Analyze Baseline Copyist Text (Strip Brackets): If you wish to calculate text metrics or execute phylogenetic splits purely on the raw copyist layer without treating these missing word repairs as variants, you can programmatically ignore them. Simply run a regex function or clean the text block to drop the brackets [ and ], merging the enclosed text directly into the pristine baseline string.

3. Quantitative Distance Metrology

Linguistic divergence is tracked through character-level **Levenshtein Distance** equations. To eliminate bias inherent to variable line lengths across transcription workflows, distances are fully normalized by dividing the raw edit tally by the character count of the longer string. The output values map the precise, localized scale of late-stage text degradation introduced by print houses.
