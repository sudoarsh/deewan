A Digital Critical & Genetic Edition

Edited by Akash Tiwari

A Structural Intervention in Textual Criticism

Mah Laqa Bai Chanda (1768–1824) was the first female poet in the history of the Urdu language to compile a complete, formal Diwan. For over 120 years, modern scholarship has misread her poetic evolution, assuming textual variations were the result of artistic instability.

This digital project deconstructs that myth. By conducting a line-by-line, word-level collation of the surviving primary manuscripts spanning twenty years of her career, this edition proves her original authorial voice was remarkably stable. The heavy variations and linguistic normalizations previously attributed to her are actually late textual corruptions introduced by an anonymous 1906 print editor. This edition performs a historical "rescue mission," bypassing a century of colonial-era institutional censorship to restore her original 18th-century Deccani synthesis.
