About the Project

This project presents a rigorous critical edition of the Diwan of Mah Laqa Bai Chanda, the pioneering 18th-century Urdu poetess. Utilizing the Lachmann method of textual criticism and structural paleography, this digital edition systematically collates multiple historical witnesses to trace the textual transmission and stylistic evolution of her ghazals.

By bringing these disparate manuscripts together into a single, navigable interface, readers and digital humanists can explore the subtle linguistic shifts that occurred during the poetess's lifetime and shortly after.

The Witnesses

The text has been transcribed and collated across four primary witnesses, representing distinct eras of the manuscript tradition:

  • BL (1799): Early manuscript housed at the British Library.
  • TO (1811): Manuscript preserved in the Telangana state archives.
  • SJ (1818): Later manuscript from the Salar Jung collection.
  • GM: A subsequent print edition serving as the base text for variant comparison and diff highlighting.

Digital Methodology

This open-access repository ensures that the raw data and the interpretive viewer remain freely available for ongoing digital humanities research. The texts have been manually transcribed, aligned at the couplet level, and dynamically rendered to highlight textual variations in real-time. Missing couplets in the earlier 1799 and 1811 manuscripts are preserved as blanks to accurately reflect the historical state of those specific codices, allowing for a transparent visual representation of the Diwan's expansion over time.
